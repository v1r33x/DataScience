Who is an

Entrepreneur ?.

business_center

Entrepreneurship is, in all sense, apposite to desire. The desire to do something, something different. The desire to change how something is done, to ease up the problems of the world and definitely the desire to earn truck loads of money. Everytime one sees a billionaire on media, say, Jeff Bezos or Mark Zuckerberg, they somehow feel like relating themselves with them. It is that desire to get the feeling of having something accomplished, that adrenaline rush, that satisfaction, which keeps one working.
Can everyone be an entrepreneur? Yes, but there is one reason that sets the successful onesapart. It's that strong desire to be successful. There are entrepreneurs who think day in and day out, “Yaar startup kholna hai, startup idea sochte hai.” "Ae aaj mil startup sochte hai.” They might even come up with a startup idea, but no one knows what their next step is, their aim is only bounded to “ startup kholna” they never think of what they have to do next.
I am of firm belief that the very first step in being a true entrepreneur is to identify a problem, and then use the resources available to solve the problem and in process make it a business. Identification of a problem and achieving the ultimate aim of solving is what should constitute an entrepreneur's mind-set. When you have the desire to solve something, you solve it. That need to reach the target and the suffocation of not having reached it yet, will keep you moving.
An entrepreneur is someone who sets out to find business opportunities and create business systems to exploit opportunities. They identify a gap, and fills it with a solution. What else motivates someone to become an entrepreneur? Money, ofcourse! The chance to earn significant profits, buy a yacht, take numerous holidays, buy designer goods and send their kids to the best private schools. Everytime one sees a picture of yacht or a luxury car, involuntarily says, “Yaar Bentley toh lunga hi”. Yes, that desire to be capable of having what you want, is one of the biggest motivator. Also, one of the biggest rewards of being an entrepreneur is the personal satisfaction that comes from having the freedom to make your own business decisions and act on them, making your own rules, doing what you enjoy and helping your community. It is the desire to succeed and fulfill your dreams, along with the ultimate aim of solving a problem and hence getting the contentment of achieving something, is what characterizes a true entrepreneur.

About Us

We at the Entrepreneurship Cell of Hindu College are a crew of driven, discerning and passionate individuals. Our club is dedicated to advancing people's understanding about entrepreneurship and diversifying their skill set by providing a deeply fulfilling and collaborative environment.
To become a competitive, robust economy in today's world one must focus on the reorganization of the skill development ecosystem and our active skill development initiatives through our skill development club helps actualizing the inert potential of the youth by expansion in skill training while also improving their quality. Since its inception in 2017 the society has tirelessly strived to bridge the gap between the skills acquired in the class and the ones relevant in todays fast changing dynamics of the world.
The social entrepreneurship wing of the E-cell is actively working towards finding innovative and sustainable solutions to the problems that plague our modern society through collaborative work with various NGO’s such as Feeding India, Greenpeace and light up.
The Hindu business review (HBR), our annual newsletter and blog is a product of our in house team keeping you updated year long with all that’s trending in the entrepreneurial ecosystem.
In a world which is constantly going from 1 to n of something we are working tirelessly and looking for people with the entrepreneurial bent and the drive to impact lives through their work to jump on board this train of ours and go from 0 to 1 for a better tomorrow!
